What you’re testing

Welcome, and thank you.

Homestead is a private coordination app for families and the caregivers they trust — grandparents, sitters, close friends. The goal: replace group texts and scheduling back-and-forth with something quiet, reliable, and built around how real help actually works.

You’re one of the first people outside the immediate family to use it. Your job is to break things, get confused, and tell us about it. That’s exactly what this phase is for.

Before you start
Read this whole document. It takes 3 minutes and will make your testing much more useful. Then open the app and follow the scenarios below.
First-time setup

Getting into the app

1
Open the link you were sent
The app lives at homestead-app-six.vercel.app. Open it in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android) — not a third-party browser.
2
Add it to your home screen
iPhone: tap the Share button → “Add to Home Screen.”   Android: tap the menu → “Add to Home Screen.” Open it from your home screen for the full experience — it behaves like a native app.
3
Sign in or create an account
Use Google or your email. The account is free — no payment info needed.
4
Accept the invite from your household
You should have received an invite link separately. Tap it — it will connect you to the right household automatically.
5
Allow push notifications when asked
The bell and shift claim features send real push notifications. Without them, you’ll miss the core experience. Tap Allow.
Your mission

Test scenarios

Work through as many of these as you can. They’re ordered by priority — the first five are the most important.

Scenario 1
Caregiver
Find and claim an open shift
Open the app. Tap the “Open Shifts” tab (first tab). You should see at least one shift from the household. Tap it to read the details, then tap “Claim this shift.”
Expected: A confirmation, the shift moves to My Schedule, and the parent receives a push notification.
Scenario 2
Caregiver
Release a shift you claimed
Open “My Schedule.” Find the shift you just claimed. Tap it, then tap “Release.” Add a reason (optional) and confirm.
Expected: Shift removed from your schedule, reopened for others, parent notified.
Scenario 3
Caregiver
Respond to a bell alert
Wait for a test bell from the parent (they’ll send one). Open the Alerts tab when you receive the push notification. Read the situation and tap one of the three response options.
Expected: Your response is recorded immediately. The bell status updates on the parent’s screen.
Scenario 4
Caregiver
Block time off
Open the “Open Shifts” tab and scroll to the bottom. Tap “+ Add” under “Not Available.” Set a date range and an optional note, then tap “Block this time.”
Expected: Your blocked time appears in the list. No error.
Scenario 5
Both roles
Push notifications arrive correctly
Have the parent post a shift (or ring the bell). Confirm you receive a push notification on your locked screen. Tap it — confirm it opens the right screen in the app.
Expected: Notification arrives within 5 seconds. Tapping it opens Open Shifts or Alerts, not the home screen.
Scenario 6
Caregiver
Close the app and reopen it
Force-close the app from your phone’s app switcher. Wait 30 seconds. Reopen it from your home screen. Navigate around all four tabs.
Expected: App loads cleanly, shows your current shift/schedule, no error banners.
Scenario 7
Caregiver
Try the detail sheet
On any shift card, tap to open the detail sheet. Try swiping it down to dismiss it. Try tapping the backdrop to dismiss it.
Expected: Both gestures close the sheet. No stuck states.
Scenario 8
Both roles
Read the user guide
Open Settings (bottom right on Village tab) → tap “How Homestead Works.” Read through it. Note anything that’s confusing, missing, or wrong.
Expected: The guide makes sense without any explanation from us.
What matters most

Things to pay attention to

Anything that blocked you
If you couldn’t complete a step, got confused about what to do next, or had to try something more than once — that’s the most valuable feedback we can get.
Push notifications
Did they arrive? How fast? Did tapping them open the right place in the app? On iOS especially, this is the part most likely to have issues.
What felt good
If something worked exactly as you’d expect, or felt surprisingly smooth, we want to know that too. It tells us what not to change.
Language and copy
Any label, button, or message that was confusing, unclear, or felt wrong. The words matter as much as the features.
Reporting issues

How to tell us what you found

Send feedback directly to mjsirmans@gmail.com or via text. Use this format so nothing gets lost:

What you were doing
e.g. “I tapped Claim on a shift in Open Shifts”
What happened
e.g. “The button went grey and nothing happened. The shift didn’t move to My Schedule.”
What you expected
e.g. “Expected a confirmation and the shift to appear in My Schedule.”
Device & browser (if known)
e.g. “iPhone 15 Pro, Safari, added to home screen”

A screenshot or screen recording is worth a thousand words. On iPhone: hold Side + Volume Up to screenshot. Press Side + Volume Up and swipe up to start a screen recording.

Important

Ground rules

This is private
Homestead is in private beta. Please don’t share the app link, screenshots, or your feedback publicly. We’ll let you know when it’s ready for the world.
This is real data
The app uses a live database. Shifts you create, claims you make, and bells you ring are real. The parent will see them. Don’t post anything you’d be embarrassed by.
You can delete your account
When you’re done testing, go to Settings → Delete Account if you want all your data removed. It’s permanent and immediate.
