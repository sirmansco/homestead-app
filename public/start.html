What you’re doing here

What is Homestead?

Homestead is a private coordination app for families who rely on a small circle of caregivers — grandparents, trusted sitters, close family friends. It replaces the group texts and scheduling back-and-forth with something quiet and reliable: parents post what they need, caregivers claim it. For urgent moments, parents ring a bell that widens through the circle until someone answers.

It’s not a marketplace. Everyone on Homestead already knows each other. This is a tool for the care relationships you’ve already built.

You’re one of the first people outside the immediate family to use it. Your job is to break things, get confused, and tell us about it. That’s exactly what this phase is for.

First-time setup

Getting into the app

1
Open the link on your phone
The app lives at homestead-app-six.vercel.app. Open it in Safari on iPhone or Chrome on Android — not a third-party browser.
2
Add to your home screen
iPhone: tap the Share button → “Add to Home Screen.”   Android: tap the menu → “Add to Home Screen.” Open from your home screen for the full experience — it behaves like a native app.
3
Sign in or create an account
Use Google or your email. Free, no payment info needed.
4
Accept your household invite
You should have received a separate invite link. Tap it — it connects you to the right household automatically. If you’re the parent setting up a fresh household, you’ll be prompted to name it and add kids.
5
Allow push notifications when asked
The bell and shift claim features send real push notifications. Without them you’ll miss the core experience. Tap Allow.
Testing the caregiver side
Go to Village → tap + → “Invite via link.” Copy the link and open it in an incognito window. Sign in with a second email — you land in the app as a caregiver. Gives you both perspectives side-by-side.
The big picture

How Homestead works

There are two roles. Parents post needs and manage the household. Caregivers claim shifts and respond to bells. Some people are both.

For Parents
Navigation

Your four tabs

📋
Almanac
Your household’s full schedule. Every shift, color-coded by status — green means covered, terracotta means still open. Your command center.
✚
Post a Need
Request coverage for a specific date and time. Add notes, set a rate, choose a specific caregiver or broadcast to the whole village. Recurring shifts supported.
🔔
Bell
For urgent situations. Ring the bell and your inner circle is notified immediately — then it widens every few minutes until someone responds.
🏘️
Village
Manage your circle. Move people between Inner Circle, Family & Friends, and Sitters to control the bell escalation order.
Urgent help

Ringing the bell

The bell is for time-sensitive situations — sick kid, last-minute conflict, pickup emergency. It’s not for advance planning (use Post for that).

1
Tap the bell icon (top right of Almanac, or the Bell tab)
Select what’s happening: Sick kid, Last-minute conflict, Pickup mixup, Emergency, or Other.
2
Set the window and add a note
When do you need someone, and until when? Add any context that helps — “fever of 101, can’t leave work.”
3
Ring
Inner circle notified instantly. If no response in 2 minutes, family & close friends are notified. After 5 minutes, trusted sitters.
When it’s resolved
Once someone’s on the way, tap “Someone is on the way · Mark handled” to silence the bell. Or cancel it from the Almanac banner without leaving your schedule view.
For Caregivers
Navigation

Your four tabs

📋
Open Shifts
All open shift requests from the families in your village. Claim one to lock it in — you’ll be the only person assigned.
📅
My Schedule
Shifts you’ve claimed. Release a shift here if something comes up — the parent is notified and it reopens for others.
🔔
Alerts
Urgent bell requests appear here instantly. Read the situation, tap one of the three response options. No response is the wrong response — “Can’t” passes it to the next circle automatically.
🏘️
Village
The families you help. Block time off from here too — families can see when you’re unavailable when planning shifts.
Blocking time

Setting yourself unavailable

If you have a stretch of time when you’re not available — vacation, work trip, anything — block it so families know not to count on you.

1
Open the Open Shifts tab and scroll to the bottom
You’ll see a “Not Available” section with a + Add button.
2
Set your dates and an optional note
e.g. “In Seattle for the week.” Families see your blocked times when reviewing who’s available.
Your mission

Test scenarios

Work through as many of these as you can, in order. The first five are the most important. Role badges show who should run each scenario.

Scenario 1
Caregiver
Find and claim an open shift
Open the app. Tap the “Open Shifts” tab. You should see at least one shift from the household. Tap it to read the details, then tap “Claim this shift.”
Expected: A confirmation, the shift moves to My Schedule, and the parent receives a push notification.
Scenario 2
Caregiver
Release a shift you claimed
Open My Schedule. Find the shift you just claimed. Tap it, then tap “Release.” Add a reason (optional) and confirm.
Expected: Shift removed from your schedule, reopened for others, parent notified.
Scenario 3
Caregiver
Respond to a bell alert
Wait for a test bell from the parent (they’ll send one). Open the Alerts tab when you receive the push. Read the situation and tap one of the three response options.
Expected: Your response is recorded immediately. Parent gets a push notification showing your answer. Bell status updates on their screen.
Scenario 4
Parent
Ring the bell and watch it escalate
From the Almanac, tap the bell icon (top right). Select a reason, set a time window, add a note, and ring. Wait 2 minutes without responding and check whether the next group gets notified.
Expected: Inner circle notified immediately. After 2 min, family & friends. After 5 min, sitters. Active bell banner appears on your Almanac.
Scenario 5
Both roles
Push notifications arrive and deep-link correctly
Have the parent post a shift (or ring the bell). Confirm you receive a push notification on your locked screen. Tap it — confirm it opens the right screen in the app.
Expected: Notification arrives within 5 seconds. Tapping it opens Open Shifts or Alerts, not the home screen.
Scenario 6
Caregiver
Block time off
Open the Open Shifts tab and scroll to the bottom. Tap “+ Add” under Not Available. Set a date range and optional note, then tap “Block this time.”
Expected: Your blocked time appears in the list. No error.
Scenario 7
Caregiver
Close the app and reopen it
Force-close the app from your phone’s app switcher. Wait 30 seconds. Reopen from your home screen. Navigate all four tabs.
Expected: App loads cleanly, shows your current shift/schedule, no error banners.
Scenario 8
Parent
Upload a photo for a village member
Open Village. Tap the small + button on any avatar. Pick a photo from your camera roll.
Expected: Photo appears on the card immediately. No error message.
Scenario 9
Caregiver
Try the detail sheet
On any shift card, tap to open the detail sheet. Try closing it by tapping the dark backdrop behind it. When the sheet is scrolled to the top, you can also swipe it down to dismiss.
Expected: Backdrop tap closes the sheet. Swipe-down dismisses when sheet is scrolled to top. Claim button works from inside the sheet.
What matters most

Things to pay attention to

Anything that blocked you
If you couldn’t complete a step, got confused about what to do next, or had to try something more than once — that’s the most valuable feedback we can get.
Push notifications
Did they arrive? How fast? Did tapping them open the right place? On iOS especially, this is the part most likely to have issues.
What felt good
If something worked exactly as you’d expect, or felt surprisingly smooth, we want to know that too. It tells us what not to change.
Language and copy
Any label, button, or message that was confusing, unclear, or felt wrong. The words matter as much as the features.
Reporting issues

How to tell us what you found

Send feedback to mjsirmans@gmail.com or via text. Use this format so nothing gets lost:

What you were doing
e.g. “I tapped Claim on a shift in Open Shifts”
What happened
e.g. “The button went grey and nothing happened. The shift didn’t move to My Schedule.”
What you expected
e.g. “Expected a confirmation and the shift to appear in My Schedule.”
Device & browser
e.g. “iPhone 15 Pro, Safari, added to home screen”

A screenshot or screen recording is worth a thousand words. iPhone: hold Side + Volume Up to screenshot. Side + Volume Up then swipe up to start a screen recording.

Important

Ground rules

This is private
Homestead is in private beta. Please don’t share the app link, screenshots, or your feedback publicly. We’ll let you know when it’s ready for the world.
This is real data
The app uses a live database. Shifts you create, claims you make, and bells you ring are real. The parent will see them. Don’t post anything you’d be embarrassed by.
You can delete your account
When you’re done, go to Village → Settings → Delete Account. It’s permanent and immediate — removes everything from the database and signs you out.
