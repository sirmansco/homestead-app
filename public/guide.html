The big picture

What is Homestead?

Homestead is a private coordination app for families who rely on a village of caregivers — grandparents, sitters, close friends — to keep life running. It’s not a marketplace. Everyone here already knows each other.

Parents post what they need. Caregivers claim it or respond to urgent bells. No group texts, no scheduling back-and-forth.

Step by step

Posting a shift

1
Tap Post (the + tab)
Fill in the shift title, date, start and end time, and optionally who it’s for (e.g. “Ellie — pickup and dinner”).
2
Set a rate (optional)
If you pay your caregivers, add an hourly rate. It shows on the shift card so everyone knows what to expect.
3
Choose who to notify
Leave it open to your whole village, or tap “Request someone specific” to send it directly to one caregiver.
4
Recurring? Toggle it on
For a weekly standing arrangement, toggle Recurring and pick the days and how many weeks. All instances post at once.
5
Tap Post
Your village is notified immediately by push notification. You’ll get a push back when someone claims it.
For urgent situations

Ringing the bell

When to ring
The bell is for time-sensitive situations — sick kid, last-minute conflict, pickup emergency. It’s not for advance planning (use Post for that).
1
Tap the bell icon (top right of Almanac)
Or navigate to the Bell tab directly.
2
Select what’s happening
Pick from: Sick kid, Last-minute conflict, Pickup mixup, Emergency, or Other.
3
Set the window
When do you need someone, and until when? Defaults to now + 3 hours.
4
Add a note (optional)
Any context that helps — “fever of 101, can’t leave work” or “school calls at 2:30”.
5
Ring
Your inner circle is notified instantly. If no one responds in 2 minutes, family and close friends are notified. After 5 minutes, trusted sitters.
When it’s resolved
Once someone is on the way, tap “Someone is on the way · Mark handled” to silence the bell and notify everyone it’s covered.
Village tab

Managing your village

The Village tab shows three circles that control bell escalation order:

Inner Circle
Notified first, immediately. Your most reliable person — a grandparent, your partner’s sister, whoever picks up every time.
Family & Close Friends
Notified 2 minutes after the inner circle if no one responds.
Trusted Sitters
Notified 5 minutes in. Paid help or neighbors you trust but call less often.

Tap a person to move them between circles. Changes take effect immediately for future bells.

Step by step

Claiming a shift

1
Open the “Open Shifts” tab
You’ll see all available shifts from families you’re connected to, sorted by soonest first.
2
Tap a shift card to read the details
Full date, time, duration, rate (if any), who it’s for, and any notes from the parent.
3
Tap “Claim this shift”
The shift is now yours. The parent gets a push notification: “Your name is on it.”
4
It moves to My Schedule
Switch to the Schedule tab to see everything you’ve committed to, sorted by date.
Can’t make it after all?
Open My Schedule, tap the shift, and tap Release. You’ll be asked for a reason — optional but appreciated. The parent is notified and the shift reopens for others.
Alerts tab

Responding to the bell

When the bell rings
You’ll get a push notification instantly. Open the Alerts tab — you’ll see the situation, the time window needed, and any note from the family.
1
Read what’s happening
The card shows the reason (e.g. “Sick kid”), any note, and the time they need coverage.
2
Choose your response
Three options: “I’m on my way” (going now), “Available in 30 min” (can help but need 30 minutes), or “Can’t — pass to next circle”.
3
The family sees your response immediately
If you said you’re on the way, the bell stops widening and the family is told. If you can’t, it moves to the next group automatically.
No guilt for “Can’t”
That’s exactly how the bell is designed. Pass it on — the system finds someone who can.
Not Available

Blocking time off

If you have a stretch of time when you’re unavailable — vacation, work trip, medical — you can block it so families know not to expect you.

1
Open Open Shifts and scroll to the bottom
You’ll see a “Not Available” section.
2
Tap “+ Add”
Set a from date/time and an until date/time. Add an optional note (e.g. “In Seattle for the week”).
3
Tap “Block this time”
It’s saved. Families can see your blocked times when reviewing who’s available.
Getting the most out of Homestead

Tips for everyone

Enable push notifications
The app is significantly less useful without them. When asked, tap Allow. You can manage notification settings in your phone’s Settings app.
Add it to your home screen
On iPhone: tap the Share button in Safari → Add to Home Screen. On Android: tap the menu → Add to Home Screen. It behaves like a native app.
Updates happen automatically
When Homestead is updated, your app refreshes silently in the background. No App Store, no manual update required.
It’s private — always
Your household is invitation-only. Nothing is public. Caregivers only see what’s relevant to them.
